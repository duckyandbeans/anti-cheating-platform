Modal
Verbs.

A one-lesson deep dive for Mrs Fenny — ten modals, what each one does, how sure or how strict it sounds, thirty practice questions, and a speaking round to finish.

01 — The Card Catalog

Ten modals, flipped.

Tap a card to flip it. The dots under each word are the Force Meter — how strong, certain, or obligatory that modal sounds. More dots, more force.

02 — Practice Drawer

30 questions.

Choose one answer per question, then press Check My Answers. Right answers turn green, wrong ones turn red, and each question gets a short note underneath.

03 — Speaking Round

Say it out loud.

Ten minutes of speaking to finish the lesson — the teacher leads, Mrs Fenny answers in full sentences using the modal each prompt is built around.

How to run this

  • Go through the Teacher Prompts one at a time — read the prompt aloud, don't show it.
  • Mrs Fenny answers in 2–3 full sentences, naturally, without writing anything down first.
  • Note any modal mix-ups quietly and circle back to them at the very end, after all ten prompts.
  • If there's time left, swap: Mrs Fenny reads a Student Prompt and free-talks for 30–60 seconds.
