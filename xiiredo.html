IV. Reading Comprehension: Cryptography

Security in the Cyber World
Cryptography involves the practice of encoding data to maintain privacy. In the modern age, it is the backbone of internet security. Without robust encryption, our digital footprints—such as passwords and credit card numbers—could be accessed by malicious actors.

There are two primary frameworks: Symmetric and Asymmetric encryption. Symmetric uses a single key for both locking and unlocking, whereas Asymmetric utilizes a pair of keys (public and private). As technology advances, so do the threats. Therefore, cryptography must constantly progress to defend against sophisticated cyber-attacks.
