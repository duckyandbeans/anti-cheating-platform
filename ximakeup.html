I. Reading Comprehension: Francisca Mardones

The Silent Strength of Francisca Mardones: A Legacy of Resilience
In the realm of Paralympic sports, Francisca Mardones stands as a beacon of excellence. Her transition from a promising tennis player to a world-record holder in shot put is more than a display of physical power; it is an masterclass in resilience and psychological fortitude. Today, Mardones is honored as a role model for individuals with disabilities worldwide, illustrating that peak performance requires as much emotional regulation as technical precision.

Success was the result of a deliberate mindset. Mardones was renowned for being remarkably mindful during competition. During her record-breaking throws, her concentration never flickered, even when facing internal doubts. She demonstrated a sophisticated level of mental clarity, remaining composed when the stakes at the Tokyo Games were at their highest. This self-confidence allowed her to surpass competitors who relied solely on raw strength but lacked her strategic focus.

Her journey was marked by significant adversity. Following a traumatic injury during a hurricane in 1999, the weight of her physical limitations could have easily eroded her self-esteem. However, her sense of belonging to the global athletic community and her unwavering grit kept her motivated. Sports historians often note that her gold medal in 2021 was secured through sheer determination, as she overcame both personal grief and a field of younger, formidable rivals.

The impact of her career transcends the medals she has won. Through her achievements, Paralympic sports in Chile were transformed into a focus of national pride. Her training methods are still analyzed by aspiring athletes, and her shot put records are unlikely to be eclipsed in the near future. Mardones’ story reminds us that while technical skill is essential, it is self-discipline and mental fortitude that turn a competitor into a lasting icon.
